Home Screen & Navigation

The home screen gives you four primary options:

  • Store page — opening hours, map, facilities, and store services
  • Language toggle — switch languages if your market has multiple options
  • Find products — opens the search and browse experience
  • Focus tile — a contextual tile configured per kiosk location (e.g. IKEA Family, planners, specific category, promotions)
💡 The focus tile can be changed in the Kiosk Admin Panel. Ask your store manager or digital champion for access.

The Store Page is a dedicated screen with store-specific information including:

  • Opening hours (synced automatically from Navigera)
  • Store map
  • IKEA Family information
  • 'How to shop' with Shop&Go guide
  • Feedback (Pulse CX module where available)
  • Second-hand corner info, Full serve order instructions

It can be accessed via the store name on the home screen, or via the Recommended tab in certain store locations.

📍 The Store Page entry point appears in: Store entrance, Showroom entrance, Market Hall entrance, Atrium, Planning area, Exit, Customer Services, and Pick-up point.

Tap the Language option on the home screen. This is only visible if your market has multiple languages configured in the system.

📋 If the language option is missing, it means only one language is set up for your store. Contact your digital champion to request additional languages.

The Focus Tile is a contextual entry point on the home screen configured per kiosk. Options include:

  • Product categories (e.g. wardrobes, kitchens)
  • Inspirational content
  • Store events
  • IKEA Family offers
  • IKEA for Business
  • Specific planners (IPEX, REX planners)
  • Promotions (NLP, undersold products)

To change it, go to the Kiosk Admin Panel — only coworkers with admin access can make changes.

Search & Browse

Tap Find products on the home screen, then tap the search button. The search bar features:

  • Keyboard opens automatically
  • Autocomplete suggestions appear as you type (words, products, categories)
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP) — type naturally, e.g. "blue sofa" or "small kitchen table"
  • Clear button to erase text
  • Enter to submit search
💡 Customers can also search for their planner using a VPC code (Planner code) in the search bar on any kiosk in the store.

From the Find products screen you can:

  • Use the Products, Rooms, Planners tabs/filters at the top
  • Swipe the horizontal scroll bar left to see more options
  • Tap a category to open subcategories
  • Tap a subcategory to see the product listing page (PLP)

The search and browse page includes a dedicated Offers tab with:

  • NLP (New Lower Price)
  • News
  • IKEA Family Offers
  • Last Chance products
  • Undersold products
  • As-is products

The Product Information Page shows:

  • Product variants (colours, sizes)
  • Product tabs with detailed information
  • Assembly instructions
  • Complementary products
  • Order products with delivery (ikea.com)
  • Entry point to planners (if product has a planner)
  • IKEA Family offers related to the product

Offers & IKEA Family

Offers are available in several places:

  • Focus tile on the home screen (if configured for offers)
  • Offers tab in the Search and browse page
  • Directly in search results and category pages

Types of offers available:

  • NLP (New Lower Price)
  • News / New arrivals
  • IKEA Family exclusive offers
  • Last chance products
  • Undersold products (slow-moving stock promoted on the focus tile)
  • As-is products

From the home screen (or via focus tile), customers can access the IKEA Family view:

  • QR code to sign up on IKEA website
  • QR code for member benefits information
  • QR code to sign in to IKEA Family
  • Browse IKEA Family exclusive offers on the Product Information Page (PIP)

To configure IKEA Family on the focus tile, go to the Admin Panel → Focus tile → IKEA Family.

IKEA for Business is accessible via the focus tile or the Recommended tab. It includes:

  • QR code to join the IKEA Business network
  • QR code for member benefits
  • QR code to book an appointment with a specialist
  • QR code to access IKEA web by sector

Enable/disable this via Admin Panel → Settings → IKEA for Business.

The All Price Offers focus tile is set as the default focus tile in the Self-Serve area. It gives customers quick access to all available offers in one place. It can also be set on other kiosk locations via the Admin Panel.

Leave & Continue (Screen Lock)

When co-workers and customers plan together (one-to-many planning), they often need to leave the kiosk to explore the physical showroom or market hall — for example to look at an actual sofa, wardrobe, or kitchen. Without the lock, the session would time out and all planning work would be lost.

The Screen Lock lets a coworker freeze the kiosk screen so the design is preserved. When the customer (and coworker) return, they unlock and continue exactly where they left off — no need to restart.

💡 This feature reduces frustration and makes the one-to-many planning process (kitchen, wardrobe, etc.) much more efficient.
Coworker feature

The Screen Lock is a hidden feature for coworkers only:

  • While on the active session/planner, long press the top-right corner of the screen (hold for a few seconds)
  • The screen will lock and display a lock screen
  • The session is preserved in the background

To unlock: tap the screen and enter the unlock action (PIN or coworker gesture — depends on your store setup).

⚠️ This is a coworker-only feature. Customers should not activate the lock themselves. Always ensure a coworker is present to unlock when returning.
Coworker

The session is preserved while the screen is locked. However, if the customer and coworker take too long to return and the lock times out, the session will end and the design will need to be restarted.

Best practice: Save the planner design with a VPC code before leaving, so even if the lock expires, the customer can return the design by entering the code.

💡 Tip: The planner VPC code is the fail-safe. Always save before exploring the store.
Coworker
Customer

Usage data is tracked in the Upptäcka Dashboard. To see how often the lock is used and how many sessions are resumed vs. restarted:

  • Access the Upptäcka Dashboard (ask your market digital champion for link)
  • Go to Items of interest or Store overview for usage KPIs
  • Contact Vasiliki (data analyst) or your market analytics team for a specific report
📊 Tracking this data helps us understand if the feature is being adopted and if coworkers need more training on when/how to use it.

Requesting Help at the Kiosk

Yes. The Request for Help feature allows customers to call a coworker directly from the kiosk. This is particularly useful during one-to-many planning sessions when a customer needs expert guidance on planning their wardrobe, kitchen, or other large solutions.

The feature is accessible directly from the kiosk interface — the customer or coworker can trigger a request for assistance.

💡 This feature is especially valuable in planning areas where customers are working on complex designs and need a coworker's help.

  • The request may appear on a coworker's device or a central display
  • The kiosk location is included so the coworker knows where to go

Contact your store's digital champion for specifics on how the help request is configured for your location.

Shop & Go Handover

After a customer selects products or completes a planner session, they can transfer their list to their mobile device via Shop & Go handover. Methods supported include:

  • QR code — customer scans with their phone
  • SMS — link sent to phone number
  • Email — list sent to email address
  • Scan & Go — direct integration with IKEA Scan & Go app

Which methods are available depends on your market's settings in the Admin Panel.

📋 Enable/disable handover methods: Admin Panel → Settings → Handover options.

Customers can find Shop & Go instructions on the Store Page. There is a dedicated card called 'How to shop with Shop&Go' that explains the process step by step.

Access: Home Screen → Store name (e.g. IKEA Malmö) → Store Page → Shop&Go card.

Customer

Upptäcka supports all main IKEA article fulfillment types:

  • Market hall article — products available to pick up directly in the market hall
  • Self serve article — customer collects from the self-serve area

  • Direct delivery article — ordered for home delivery via ikea.com

Price Tags & In-Store Web

Price Tags in IKEA stores display product information including price, article number, and features. The in-store web and Upptäcka kiosk complement the price tag experience:

  • Customers can scan QR codes on price tags to access the product page on Upptäcka or the IKEA website
  • Discovered product information flows between price tags and the Upptäcka PIP (Product Information Page)
  • Article types (market hall, self serve, full serve, direct delivery) shown on Ptags align with Upptäcka's buying options
📋 The Ptag use case connects physical discovery (price tags in the store) with digital planning (Upptäcka kiosk or in-store web).

Local PTags (SPRs) allow individual stores to create customized price tag content for specific articles. This enables market- or store-specific pricing and promotions to be displayed on the Upptäcka kiosk and in-store web.

Coworker · Store admin

In-Store Web is a web-based version of Upptäcka accessible from mobile browsers in the store. It offers a similar experience to the kiosk but on the customer's own device.

  • Customers can access it by scanning a QR code in the store
  • The feedback module was introduced on Instore Web before the kiosk version
  • Useful for stores with fewer kiosks or for customers who prefer their own device

Troubleshooting & Support

If a kiosk has an issue:

  • Log in to the Service Portal (Now IT)
  • Search for "Upptäcka"
  • Fill in the issue template provided

You can also check the overall kiosk status via the Upptäcka Console → Status page (shows outages and incidents with resolution status).

⚠️ Do not try to fix hardware issues yourself. Always log a ticket in Now IT first.
Coworker

Kiosk opening hours are automatically synced from Navigera. To fix them:

  • Log in to Navigera
  • Update the opening hours there
  • Changes will be automatically reflected in Upptäcka
⚠️ Do NOT try to change the hours directly in Upptäcka — they will be overwritten by Navigera's data.
Coworker · Navigera access needed

Use the Upptäcka Console:

  • Welcome page — overview of all kiosks, operational vs. unconfigured count, graph of operational status
  • Status page — overall operational status, outages, and incidents with resolution status
  • Market view — kiosk list, store formats, add-ons enabled per market
  • Store view — move or delete kiosks, store-level summary
  • Settings — view all integrations and add-ons enabled per market
